What Is an Unvented Hot Water Cylinder?

While your boiler is the heart of your home’s water system, it relies on another crucial component: the hot water cylinder. There are two primary types of hot water cylinders: vented and unvented. Each has its own advantages and disadvantages, but many homeowners and businesses prefer unvented water cylinders when considering new boiler installations.

Why Choose an Unvented Water Cylinder Install Surrey?

  • Direct water supply from the mains, offering stronger water pressure
  • No water tank required, allowing for flexible installation locations
  • More space-efficient and future-proof technology
  • Built-in safety features for peace of mind
  • Longer lifespan compared to vented hot water cylinders

However, it’s important to note that unvented water cylinders typically have higher installation and maintenance costs compared to vented cylinders.

How Do Unvented Water Cylinders Work?

Unvented hot water cylinders draw their water supply directly from the mains, rather than relying on a separate storage tank. This direct connection results in stronger water pressure compared to vented hot water cylinders.

Unvented cylinders also come with built-in safety features, offering homeowners and businesses additional peace of mind. Their tankless design makes them more space-efficient, allowing for installation in various locations within a property.
